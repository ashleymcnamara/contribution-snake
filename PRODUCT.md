# Product

## Register

product

## Users

GitSnake is for developers and GitHub community members who want a delightful, immediately understandable break. They may arrive to play their own contribution history, compare a Daily result, chase a Classic score, or explore the contribution patterns of open-source maintainers.

## Product Purpose

GitSnake turns the familiar GitHub contribution graph into a personal, replayable game. Its strongest experience makes a player's own work history feel alive; Daily and Classic extend that emotional pull through fair competition, progression, and shared rituals. Success means the game is inviting within seconds, rewarding across repeated sessions, and distinctive enough to feel inseparable from GitHub culture.

## Brand Personality

Playful, polished, GitHub-native. The voice is warm and clever without becoming cutesy or noisy. Delight should come from the expressive snake, contribution-grid mechanics, and well-timed Git metaphors rather than ornamental interface chrome.

## Anti-references

GitSnake must not resemble a generic AI-generated SaaS dashboard: no interchangeable card grids, decorative metrics, glass panels, gratuitous gradients, or enterprise-product language. It should also avoid overstimulating arcade clutter, novelty-terminal cosplay, and a literal reproduction of GitHub's interface.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the graph be the protagonist.** The contribution board and snake carry the identity; surrounding UI should frame play rather than compete with it.
2. **Make depth progressive.** Starting a run stays immediate while campaigns, objectives, standings, and cosmetics reveal themselves at the moment they become useful.
3. **Earn every Git metaphor.** Rebase, Fork, Squash, commits, and streaks should clarify mechanics or add personality—not become labels pasted onto ordinary UI.
4. **Use delight as feedback.** Motion, sound, copy, and expression should celebrate meaningful actions and state changes, never decorate idle screens by reflex.
5. **Keep competition legible and fair.** Objectives, rank, progress, power-ups, and ranked-versus-unranked rules must remain understandable at a glance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve complete keyboard operation, screen-reader announcements, visible focus, sufficient text and control contrast, reduced-motion alternatives, touch-friendly targets, light and dark themes, and the colorblind-safe palette. Gameplay meaning must never depend on color alone.
